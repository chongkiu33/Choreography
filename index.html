1.Characterless
The dancer are all ordinary people dress in ordinary clothes. Three men and four women dressed in casual clothes typical of late 1960 Americans: shirts, t-shirts, jeans / loose trousers and sneakers. One ’magic-man’ wearing a top hat.
2.Disoriented
In the Trio A, the choreography look like a disoriented body.
3.Disharmony
The body language is in between harmony and disharmony. There is a set of movements where the body is constantly shaking and the arms are swinging back and forth. There are movements that look like an imitation of a machine. The dance always continues in an unexpected way.
4.Discrete movement:
Trio A consists of a four-and-a-half-to-five-minute sequence of discrete movements that with the exception of walking.
5.Experimental
Trio A was initially performed in 1966 by Rainer and fellow New York choreographer-dancers under the title The Mind is a muscle at Manhattan’s Judson Memorial Church. After that. It has been presented in various forms. Sometimes integrated into other pieces by Rainer or adapted by other choreographers.
6.Lack of interaction
Performer never makes eye contact with spectators, and in the instance in which the movement requires the dancer to face the audience, the eyes are closed or the head is involved in movement. This create the effect of a blank gaze in to the mid-ground.
7.Machine-like
8.Minimalist Rainer transposed to dance the ideas that were then giving shape to the era’s minimalist sculpture and painting, abandoning the aesthetic of classical and moderndance.
9.Neutral.
10.Non-repeat
Although Rainer used repetition in earlier works as a device to make movement easier to read, she decided to not repeat movement in Trio A.
11.Sound-less
Even though Rainer play with sound in her early work, there is no sound and rhythm in Trio A. She remove the syncopation in the choreography in order to make it no dramatic.
12.Task-oriented
Rainer execute movements with an even distribution of energy reflected a challenge to traditional attitudes to “phrasing”, which can be defined as the way in which energy investment within both a given phrase and the transition from one to another.
13.Unique
The Mind is A muscle :TrioA is a sets of unique dance. I can’t tell the meaning behind the dance when I first look at it.

C. Position of The mind is a muscle
NO to spectacle.
No to virtuosity.
No to transformations and magic and make-believe.
No to the glamour and transcendency of the star image.
No to the heroic.
No to the anti-heroic.
No to trash imagery.
No to involvement of performer or spectator.
No to style.
No to camp.
No to seduction of spectator by the wiles of the performer.
No to eccentricity.
No to moving or being moved.
Rainer was trained in a style of movement characterized by expressiveness and virtuosity and in narrative choreography filled with drama and psychological intensity. He began to dissatisfied with the conventions of modern dance and the traditional relationship between dancer and audience. “Early on, I began to question the pleasure I took in being looked at, this dual voyeuristic, exhibitionistic relation of dancer to audience”Fueled by such questioning, and her opposition to the tenets of classical and modern dance, she created Trio A.
D. Similarity and Different
1.Work
My project and The Mind is a Muscle both talk about body performance at work. Rainer introduce many work-like performances into her choreography in order to propose a new form of non stylish dance. This inspire me to use dance as way to propose a new form of work. Can we consider working as entertainment/leisure when we are dancing?
2.Body language
The body language of Trio A features a seamless flow of everyday movements like toe tapping, walking, and kneeling.
3.Challenge
Rainer’s work is challenge the modern dance and the tradition relationship between dancer and audience.
4.Choreography
The way Rainer directed movement proposed that choreography might be a kind of readymade aesthetic to be ‘found’ in city street, which encourage me to find choreography in working environment.
5.Humor
In work of Rainer, presentation of the body-as-object or object-as-body tested a continuum between inertia a movement or between the individual and the industrial, often with humor. In my project, I next want to use my body to explore the boundaries between productivity and laziness, work and leisure with a sense of humor.
6.Disorientated
Like the body in Rainer's work, there was sometimes a sense of disorientation in the body at work in my research.
7.Natural
